Melbourne POI

Block Arcade

The Block Arcade was built in 1892 and modelled on the Galleria Vittoria Emanuele in Milan, it is a heritage listed shopping arcade situated on Melbourne's Golden Mile and home to the largest mosaic floor in the southern hemisphere, its magnificent glass dome and the iconic Hopetoun Tearooms.

Fitzroy Gardens

Fitzroy Gardens is one of Melbourne's most historic and beautiful Gardens. Originally set aside as a reserve in 1848, the layout follows a classic Victorian era design, featuring pathways lined with magnificent Elm trees, and a variety of flowers, ornamental shrubs and trees which together with extensive lawns create a diverse and layered landscape.

Southbank

Lovely riverside along the Yarra River in Melbourne with lots of nice cafes and restaurants and shopping areas.

Hardware Lane

Hardware Lane is a great atmospheric place to eat and drink - many varied restaurants and cafes, to suit most tastes and budgets.

Collins Street

The heart of Melbourne, Collins Street offers you plenty of options for shopping, people watching, strolls, window shopping, sightseeing et al. Great place to get a real pulse of the city.

Royal Exhibition Building

The Royal Exhibition Building is one of the world's oldest remaining exhibition pavilions. On 1 July 2004, it became the first building in Australia to achieve a World Heritage listing. Located in Carlton Gardens, the pavilion was originally completed in 1880 for the first of two international fairs hosted by Melbourne.

Melbourne Town Hall

Completed in 1870, the Melbourne Town Hall is at the heart of the city's cultural and civic activity, hosting theatre, weddings, receptions and exhibitions. So many of the decisions that have helped shape Melbourne were made within the heritage-listed Town Hall.

Lygon Street

Lygon Street is very Melbourne - the people, the shops, the restaurants, the bookshops, the cinemas - Cinema Nova. It's easy to find a good place to eat down here - just follow the crowds.

Hosier Lane

Full of discovery and delight, Hosier Lane teases the imagination long after you've put your weary feet up to rest. They weave through the heart of the city: cafes, bars, restaurants and boutiques, throbbing with conversation and espresso coffee.

Flinders Street Station

Flinders Street Station, first built in 1910, is the first station in Australia! Fantastic place to explore the city from- easy walking distance to many sights and landmarks.
